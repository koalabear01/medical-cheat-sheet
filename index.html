Liver Function Test (LFT) / Liver Panel

▶
Parameter Normal Range (Adults) Common Interpretations
Alanine Aminotransferase (ALT/SGPT) < 50 U/L (some labs < 36 U/L)
  • High: Liver cell damage (hepatitis, fatty liver, drug-induced liver injury)
Aspartate Aminotransferase (AST/SGOT) < 50 U/L (some labs < 35 U/L)
  • High: Liver cell damage, muscle injury, heart attack
Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP) 40-150 U/L (varies by age/sex)
  • High: Bile duct obstruction, bone diseases, pregnancy
Total Bilirubin 0.3-1.2 mg/dL
  • High: Jaundice, liver disease, red blood cell breakdown
Direct (Conjugated) Bilirubin 0.0-0.3 mg/dL
  • High: Bile duct obstruction, impaired bilirubin excretion
Indirect (Unconjugated) Bilirubin 0.1-1.0 mg/dL
  • High: Hemolysis, Gilbert's syndrome
Total Protein 6.0-8.3 g/dL
  • High: Dehydration, chronic inflammation
  • Low: Liver disease, kidney disease, malnutrition
Albumin 3.5-5.0 g/dL
  • High: Dehydration
  • Low: Liver disease, kidney disease, malnutrition, chronic inflammation
Globulin 2.0-3.5 g/dL (Calculated: Total Protein - Albumin)
  • High: Chronic infection, autoimmune diseases
  • Low: Immunodeficiency
Gamma-Glutamyl Transferase (GGT) 9-48 U/L
  • High: Liver disease (especially with alcohol or certain medications), bile duct issues

Kidney Function Test (KFT) / Renal Panel

▶
Parameter Normal Range (Adults) Common Interpretations
Blood Urea Nitrogen (BUN) 7-20 mg/dL
  • High: Kidney dysfunction, dehydration, high protein diet
  • Low: Liver disease, malnutrition, overhydration
Creatinine (Cr) Men: 0.7-1.3 mg/dL
Women: 0.6-1.1 mg/dL
  • High: Kidney dysfunction, muscle damage
  • Low: Low muscle mass, liver disease
eGFR (Estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate) > 60 mL/min/1.73 mÂ²
  • Low: Kidney disease/impairment
Sodium (Na+) 135-145 mEq/L
  • High (Hypernatremia): Dehydration, diabetes insipidus
  • Low (Hyponatremia): Overhydration, heart failure, kidney disease, certain medications
Potassium (K+) 3.5-5.0 mEq/L
  • High (Hyperkalemia): Kidney failure, acidosis, certain medications
  • Low (Hypokalemia): Diarrhea, vomiting, diuretic use, alkalosis
Chloride (Cl-) 96-106 mEq/L
  • High (Hyperchloremia): Dehydration, kidney disease, certain medications
  • Low (Hypochloremia): Vomiting, overhydration, heart failure
Bicarbonate (HCO3-) / CO2 22-29 mEq/L
  • High (Metabolic Alkalosis): Vomiting, diuretic use
  • Low (Metabolic Acidosis): Kidney disease, diabetic ketoacidosis, diarrhea
Calcium (Ca) 8.5-10.5 mg/dL
  • High (Hypercalcemia): Hyperparathyroidism, cancer
  • Low (Hypocalcemia): Hypoparathyroidism, vitamin D deficiency, kidney failure
Phosphorus (P) 2.5-4.5 mg/dL
  • High (Hyperphosphatemia): Kidney failure, hypoparathyroidism
  • Low (Hypophosphatemia): Malnutrition, vitamin D deficiency, hyperparathyroidism
Uric Acid Men: 3.5-7.2 mg/dL
Women: 2.6-6.0 mg/dL
  • High: Gout, kidney disease, certain medications, high purine diet

Lipid Profile

▶
Parameter Normal Range (Adults) Common Interpretations
Total Cholesterol < 200 mg/dL (Desirable)
  • High: Increased risk of cardiovascular disease
LDL Cholesterol (Bad Cholesterol) < 100 mg/dL (Optimal)
  • High: Increased risk of atherosclerosis and heart disease
HDL Cholesterol (Good Cholesterol) > 60 mg/dL (Optimal)
  • Low: Increased risk of cardiovascular disease
Triglycerides < 150 mg/dL (Normal)
  • High: Increased risk of heart disease, pancreatitis

Thyroid Profile

▶
Parameter Normal Range (Adults) Common Interpretations
TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone) 0.4-4.0 mIU/L (some labs 0.4-4.5)
  • High: Hypothyroidism (underactive thyroid)
  • Low: Hyperthyroidism (overactive thyroid)
Free T4 (Free Thyroxine) 0.8-1.8 ng/dL
  • High: Hyperthyroidism
  • Low: Hypothyroidism
Free T3 (Free Triiodothyronine) 2.3-4.2 pg/mL
  • High: Hyperthyroidism
  • Low: Hypothyroidism

Blood Sugar Tests

▶
Parameter Normal Range (Adults) Common Interpretations
Fasting Blood Sugar (FBS) < 100 mg/dL
  • Prediabetes: 100-125 mg/dL
  • Diabetes: ≥ 126 mg/dL (on two separate occasions)
Random Blood Sugar < 140 mg/dL
  • Diabetes: ≥ 200 mg/dL (with symptoms)
HbA1c (Glycated Hemoglobin) < 5.7%
  • Prediabetes: 5.7% - 6.4%
  • Diabetes: ≥ 6.5%

Inflammatory Markers

▶
Parameter Normal Range (Adults) Common Interpretations
C-Reactive Protein (CRP) < 1.0 mg/dL (or < 10 mg/L)
  • High: Inflammation, infection, tissue injury, cardiovascular risk
Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate (ESR) Men < 50 years: < 15 mm/hr
Men ≥ 50 years: < 20 mm/hr
Women < 50 years: < 20 mm/hr
Women ≥ 50 years: < 30 mm/hr
  • High: Inflammation, infection, autoimmune diseases, certain cancers
  • *Note: Affected by age and sex.*

Vitamin Levels

▶
Parameter Normal Range (Adults) Common Interpretations
Vitamin D (25-hydroxyvitamin D) Sufficient: ≥ 30 ng/mL (75 nmol/L)
Optimal: 40-60 ng/mL (100-150 nmol/L)
  • Deficient: < 20 ng/mL (50 nmol/L)
  • Insufficient: 20-29 ng/mL (50-74 nmol/L)
Vitamin B12 200 - 900 pg/mL (ranges can vary)
  • Low: Deficiency (can cause anemia, neurological issues)
  • *Note: Active B12 (holotranscobalamin) is a more accurate marker.*

Arterial Blood Gas (ABG)

▶
Parameter Normal Range (Adults) Common Interpretations
pH 7.35 - 7.45
  • < 7.35: Acidosis
  • > 7.45: Alkalosis
PaO2 (Partial pressure of oxygen) 75 - 100 mmHg
  • Low: Hypoxemia (respiratory failure, lung disease)
PaCO2 (Partial pressure of carbon dioxide) 35 - 45 mmHg
  • < 35 mmHg: Respiratory Alkalosis (hyperventilation)
  • > 45 mmHg: Respiratory Acidosis (hypoventilation)
HCO3- (Bicarbonate) 22 - 26 mEq/L
  • < 22 mEq/L: Metabolic Acidosis
  • > 26 mEq/L: Metabolic Alkalosis
SaO2 (Oxygen Saturation) 95% - 100%
  • Low: Hypoxemia

Cardiac Markers (Acute Events)

▶

These markers are primarily used in acute settings (e.g., emergency department) to diagnose myocardial injury (heart attack).

Parameter Normal Range (Adults) Common Interpretations
Troponin I / T Very low, often below detection limit (e.g., < 0.04 ng/mL, varies by lab/assay)
  • Any detectable rise or rise above specific cutoff: Indicates myocardial injury (e.g., heart attack)
Creatine Kinase-MB (CK-MB) < 3-5 ng/mL or < 5% of total CK (values vary by lab)
  • High: Myocardial injury (less specific than troponin, also found in skeletal muscle injury)

Urine Routine & Microscopic Examination

▶

Urine routine examination assesses the physical, chemical, and microscopic properties of urine.

Physical Examination

Parameter Normal Finding Common Interpretations
Color Pale yellow to Amber
  • Abnormal: Dark yellow, orange, red, brown, cloudy (hydration, medications, infection)
Clarity/Appearance Clear
  • Abnormal: Hazy, cloudy, turbid (infection, crystals, cells)
Specific Gravity 1.005-1.030
  • High: Dehydration, concentrated urine
  • Low: Overhydration, kidney disease, diabetes insipidus
pH 4.5-8.0 (Average 6.0)
  • Abnormal: Highly acidic/alkaline (diet, infection, kidney stones)

Chemical Examination (Dipstick)

Parameter Normal Finding Common Interpretations
Protein Negative
  • Positive: Kidney damage, infection, inflammation, exercise
Glucose Negative
  • Positive: Diabetes mellitus, kidney tubule damage
Ketones Negative
  • Positive: Diabetic ketoacidosis, starvation, low-carb diet
Blood Negative
  • Positive: Kidney stones, UTI, kidney injury, menstruation
Nitrite Negative
  • Positive: Bacterial infection (UTI)
Leukocyte Esterase Negative
  • Positive: Infection (presence of WBCs)
Bilirubin Negative
  • Positive: Liver disease, bile duct obstruction
Urobilinogen Small amount (0.2-1.0 mg/dL)
  • High: Liver disease, hemolytic anemia
  • Low/Absent: Bile duct obstruction

Microscopic Examination

Parameter Normal Finding Common Interpretations
RBCs 0-2/HPF (High Power Field)
  • Increased: Kidney stones, UTI, kidney injury, glomerulonephritis
WBCs 0-5/HPF
  • Increased: Infection (UTI), inflammation
Epithelial Cells Few
  • Increased: Contamination, inflammation of urinary tract
Casts Absent (Hyaline casts, few, may be normal)
  • Present: Kidney disease (e.g., RBC casts in glomerulonephritis, WBC casts in pyelonephritis)
Crystals Few (e.g., Calcium Oxalate, Uric Acid)
  • Increased: Kidney stones, metabolic disorders, diet
Bacteria/Yeast Absent
  • Present: Infection (UTI)
